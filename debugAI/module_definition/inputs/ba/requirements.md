# Requirements Baseline

## Domain Summary
The system manages **insurance claims** through intake, assignment, review, decisioning, payout, and participant notifications.

## Business Problem And Goals

### Business Problem
The organization needs a structured claims workflow that reduces manual handling, enforces review controls, and maintains compliance under high document volume.

### Business Goals
- Improve claim intake consistency
- Reduce delays caused by incomplete submissions
- Enforce escalation for high-value or risky claims
- Maintain auditable claim history
- Support timely notifications to participants

## Actors And Roles
| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Claimant | Submit claim and provide requested information |
| Claims Agent | Review claims, request information, calculate payout, and make or recommend decisions |
| Supervisor | Approve escalated claims and oversee exceptions |

## Workflow Baseline
1. Claimant submits a claim.
2. System validates required fields and documents for claim type.
3. Claim is assigned to a claims agent.
4. Agent reviews the claim and supporting information.
5. If information is missing, the agent requests more information.
6. If fraud indicators are present, the claim enters additional review.
7. If the claim meets the large-claim threshold, supervisor approval is required.
8. Claim is approved or denied.
9. If approved, payout is calculated.
10. Notifications are sent at key workflow points.

### Claim Lifecycle Statuses
`Submitted -> Assigned -> Under Review -> Pending Information -> Fraud Review -> Approved/Denied -> Paid -> Closed`

## Functional Scope Baseline
| Capability | Baseline Interpretation |
